Welcome to the Project

Empowering Research through Open-Source Bibliographic Management

WebApp Github

VaultBiblio is a revolutionary, open-source platform designed to simplify and enhance the way researchers manage their references. Whether you’re an individual academic, part of a collaborative research group, or involved in large-scale projects, VaultBiblio offers a flexible and scalable solution to streamline your bibliographic workflows.

In today’s research landscape, tools for managing citations and references are essential, yet many existing solutions are siloed, proprietary, or restrictive—often requiring expensive licenses or limiting access to shared data. At VaultBiblio, we believe researchers must be independent of costly, restrictive tools that impede the freedom to explore and pursue knowledge.

As part of our commitment to supporting open research, VaultBiblio is fully community-driven, allowing for seamless collaboration and innovation.

Key Features

  • Open-Source Platform: VaultBiblio’s open-source nature encourages contributions from developers, researchers, and users worldwide, ensuring continuous improvement and customization.
  • Interoperability: Easily import and export reference data across multiple formats (JSON, CSV, Excel) and other reference management tools like Zotero, Mendeley, and EndNote.
  • AI Integration: Use AI-powered features like reference predictions and OCR extraction from images to automate tedious tasks and enhance your workflow.
  • Collaborative Database Sharing: Build, share, and refine reference databases with colleagues, researchers, and the global academic community.
  • User-Focused Development: VaultBiblio is designed for the researcher first, prioritizing ease of use, flexibility, and data privacy.

VaultBiblio is not just a tool; it’s a movement to give researchers the tools they need to take control of their work, break down barriers, and drive forward their academic pursuits with confidence. Join us in creating a future of open, collaborative, and efficient research.
